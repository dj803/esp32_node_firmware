# 2026-04-27 — Cascade-fix marathon (v0.4.13 → v0.4.20)

One-day session that closed a multi-week panic-cascade issue, shipped 7
firmware versions, captured 8 future-improvement entries, and produced
the chaos-test framework + autonomous-prompt template the project will
use going forward.

---

## Headline

**The "v0.4.10 #51 fleet panic" was not the AsyncTCP race we initially
thought.** The actual trigger: `MQTT_HUNG_TIMEOUT_MS = 12 000` was shorter
than lwIP's TCP SYN timeout (~75 s), so any broker outage > 12 s caused
every fleet device to `ESP.restart()` simultaneously. The AsyncTCP
`tcp_arg` panic captured at 14:04 SAST was a SECONDARY effect of the
restart-storm, not the cause.

The diagnosis took most of the afternoon — multiple wrong fork swaps
(esphome v2.1.4 unbuildable, esphome v2.0.1 boot-loops, mathieucarbou
v3.3.2 stable but only changed the symptom shape) before we noticed the
firmware-side timeout. Once recognised, the fix was a 1-line `90 000`
in `config.h`. That fixed M2 (≤ 75 s outages). v0.4.16 added the broker
probe to fix M3 (> 75 s outages) entirely.

## Lessons

1. **Diagnostic-first wins.** The 14:04 backtrace capture (Charlie serial, no-DTR mode) cracked the case. Without it we'd have shipped a wrong fix and recurred.
2. **Symptom shape ≠ cause.** Three forks of AsyncTCP all eventually traced back to a firmware-side trigger. Library-level bugs are rare; library-level symptoms are common.
3. **Timeouts have hierarchies.** A firmware-level timeout shorter than the underlying network-stack timeout is almost always wrong. lwIP TCP SYN ~75 s, our `mqttIsHung` was 12 s — guaranteed misfire.
4. **Pre-flight probes ARE worth it.** The broker probe (`#67` option C, `~30` lines) eliminated the entire class of long-outage cascades. The library bug is still there; we just don't trigger it.
5. **Persistent diagnostic state.** Coredump-to-flash + next-boot publish (`#65`) means a remote panic surfaces its backtrace automatically. The "no backtrace captured" gap that cost 2 hours today cannot recur.
6. **Templates beat ad-hoc prompts.** The `AUTONOMOUS_PROMPT_TEMPLATE.md` pattern was born mid-session and used for the second half — visibly improved both the agent's pace and the operator's confidence.
